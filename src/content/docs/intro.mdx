## What makes Kando so efficient?

Kando uses [Fitts's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fitts%27s_law) to make selections as fast as possible.
In additions, Kando is a [_marking menu_](https://www.research.autodesk.com/app/uploads/2023/03/the-design-and-evaluation.pdf_recHpUp1v9dc1n2CJ.pdf) which allows you to select items by drawing zig-zag lines.

<details>
<summary>What is Fitts's Law?</summary>

Fitts's Law tells us that the time required to move to a target primarily depends on the distance to the target and the size of the target in direction of the movement.
**A pie menu minimizes the distance to the target and maximizes the size of the target!**
You do not have to aim at the items you want to select, but you can click anywhere in the wedge of an item!

</details>

This is extremely powerful!
**You can select things in subsubsubmenus in well below a second without looking at your screen!**

## Which input devices can I use with Kando?

Kando is designed to be used with 🖱️ mouse, 🖊️ stylus, 👆 touch, or 🎮 controller input.
However, since version 2.3.0, Kando also brings decent keyboard support!
Now, you can assign letters to each item.
This way, you can quickly select items in submenus without having to move your hand to the mouse!
